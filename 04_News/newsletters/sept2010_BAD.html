September 2010 PiE Newsletter
Our first  newsletter of the new school year, with a fresh perspective from our Superintendent!
PiE Makes District More Effective,
Says Superintendent Skelly
At the first School Board meeting of the year, Superintendent Kevin Skelly announced
higher than expected enrollment growth, an unanticipated outcome that may alter
current and future budget plans.  Preliminary estimates show enrollment is up more
than 200 students, mostly at the elementary school level.  "We budgeted conservatively
this year," Skelly says, "but the future looks challenging.  We just couldn't make
it without the support that we have from PiE."
PiE dollars are an increasingly important part of the district's overall budget
plan.  "PiE remains indispensible in our efforts to be responsive to the community,"
Skelly says.  "PiE listens to parents about what's important, they distill that
information, and then we have a conversation that informs our work."  The feedback
loop between educators, parents, district staff, and PiE has made the district as
a whole more effective, Skelly asserts.  Communication between parents and the district
has increased, principals are supported with full site discretionary funding to
respond to specific staff needs, and donors feel confident that the district is
using their donations to enhance student achievement.  "PiE donations, and the
structure
we have around soliciting parent support, reflect that full sense of community that
comes from being part of the whole district," says Skelly.
"It's a message we give to each of our principals as well.  We tell them to think
about our schools as deeply interdependent -- our success is dependent on our close
work together." Skelly is still waiting for the state budget crisis to be resolved.
 But he is optimistic about the current financial situation for the Palo Alto Unified
School District in the coming year.  "The uncertainties that we faced a year ago
 are still there," says Skelly.  "But I think we've done a lot of things that will
put us in a good position going forward."  This includes the recent parcel tax,
local property tax revenue, and ongoing parent and community support.  Skelly
anticipates
these revenue streams will help mitigate the district's use of the undesignated
reserve and give the district more flexibility, even with the uncertainties in state
funding.
Skelly believes that careful financial planning has helped the district weather
the current economic recession, but he warns that, "the dark clouds haven't gone
 away.  It still feels like it's raining.  We've got pretty good levees here, but
I don't think we're fully through the storm.  And I don't see any scenario in which
the work of PiE becomes any less important just because we've been good stewards
 of our financial resources in this past year."
