Magic in the Middle:  PiE Dollars Make PAUSD Middle Schools Marvelous!

  
 

PiE dollars help schools at every level in PAUSD, but they are playing an increasingly pivotal role in our middle schools.  From innovative new systems of counseling, to essential technology mentoring for teachers and critical electives support, middle school staff and teachers rely on parent donations to achieve excellence at the middle school level.

 

For the first time, PiE funds have made it possible for all three Palo Alto Unified School District Middle Schools to dedicate one counselor per grade level.  This counselor then migrates with students as they progress from sixth through eighth grade.  Bhavna Narula, current eighth-grade counselor at Terman, is thrilled with the new process.  "PiE dollars have made my work more meaningful.  It makes more sense as a counselor to be able to loop with the students and provide consistency.  Students feel more comfortable sharing with me, rather than getting adjusted to a new person every grade level." 

 

And there are other advantages to the new system.  Kerri Smith, eighth-grade counselor at Jordan, believes it is a huge benefit for parents as well.  "This model affords us important bonding with students and families from the outset, and that adds to the overall quality of experience for everyone."  Grateful parents agree: "These days with cyber-bullying and other, more subtle forms of social alienation, I am so comforted to know that there are professionals at Jordan who are keeping a watchful eye on my child, getting to know him better each year, and supporting him through the many challenges he faces," said one.

 

There are benefits for teachers too, Terman counselor Narula adds, because counselors have a "warehouse of knowledge" that they can share about a particular student from year to year.   For example, if a student needs academic support at Terman, the counselor may recommend a Focus on Success class, also paid for with PiE dollars.  These classes, taught during the school day by PAUSD teachers, support students' academic success and build strong study skills, critical to both middle school and high school achievement.

 

This year, PiE funds have also made a difference for middle school electives.  PiE funds chorus programs at all three middle schools that wouldn't exist otherwise.  Sarah Cane, Jordan's chorus teacher, believes that when students gain confidence in their musical ability, it can positively affect academic outcomes as well.  She has witnessed transformations in students from shy, unsure, self-conscious non-musicians to sweet-sounding, self-confident soloists over the course of one year.  With PiE support, she was able to add a new elective this year in advanced choral music for 7th and 8th graders.  "PiE has actually saved our electives program," remarks Terman Principal Baker.  "And these are the classes kids need.  They're fun, they're hands-on...it makes for well-rounded students.  We're a high performing school district, and the electives help everyone shine."

 

Helping our teachers shine are two TOSA (Teachers on Special Assignment) specialists, Karin Forsell and James Sperry, technology mentors at JLS Middle School.  PiE donations enable these PAUSD teachers to support colleagues as they navigate the ever-changing technology landscape.  For example, a language arts teacher may want students to digitally record skits they perform, but needs a submission process for the final product.  Or a history teacher with no blogging experience may want students to start a blog about a hypothetical historical event. Forsell and Sperry are often called in to brainstorm the parameters and possibilities of teacher and staff requests, or simply act as a guest teacher for a unit on computer science.  Both also serve on a technology committee at JLS, assist administrators with technology solutions, and install and support new equipment. 

 

PiE dollars work hard in our district middle schools to support student achievement by providing critical counseling services, technology support for teachers, and class electives that help each of our middle school students reach their full potential.  Please consider a donation to PiE today to support these efforts.

PiE Middle School Tours Introduce Parents to their Child's Next School

 

Last week, nearly 200 parents took advantage of PiE's invitation to tour one of our three middle schools.  Informal and informative, the PIE middle school tours introduced parents to their child's next schools, with campus tours, information sessions, and very brief presentations by PiE Board members.  Parents welcomed the opportunity to get this early look at next year!  PiE is deeply grateful to the staff and administration of the PAUSD middle schools that helped make these tours possible.  Thanks to all who participated!
