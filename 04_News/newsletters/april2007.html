    • Mayfield Mortgage Chips in for PiE

    Carl King, Proprietor of Mayfield Mortgage in Palo Alto, supports Palo Alto Partners in Education (PiE) both as a parent -- he has two children at Duveneck Elementary School -- but also as a local business owner and community member.

    "I believe in supporting local businesses to the extent possible and in exchange local businesses should support the community. It can start a cycle of reciprocity, creating a win-win situation for all," King said.

    Mayfield Mortgage contributed $1,000 this year to PiE's Corporate Friends Program and also donates $300 to PiE for all new mortgage clients and $150 for all existing clients who close a mortgage with Mayfield and request a donation to be made to PiE.

    "I think that one must give something, whether volunteering time or giving money, to be part of a community," he said. "Supporting PiE is one part of that community-building effort to me."

    For information on Mayfield Mortgage contact King at 650.494.7470 x101 or ck3@mayfieldmortgage.com. Or visit Mayfield Mortgage


    Frisbees and Beach Balls for PiE

    [Your Brand Here] is a promotional products agency. They provide logo merchandise, apparel and services (artwork, idea development etc.) to companies, individuals, schools and organizations. Last year Christy Wolf, owner of [Your Brand Here] in Palo Alto and a PAUSD mom, donated 100 Frisbees to the PiE-sponsored May Fete/Town Fair event. This May they will be contributing 700 beach balls.

    "[Your Brand Here] is run by mothers who have children in local elementary schools," Wolf said. "We are all active volunteers at the schools our children attend. Our involvement at our children's schools gives us a first-hand look at the benefit of PiE and also reinforces in our mind the need to support a well- rounded education - we achieve this by supporting local organizations like PiE."

    "A lot of business owners don't connect the youth in our communities to their own success. Businesses are held afloat by students and their families through daily commerce, and these students are in reality the future 'staff' of our city."

    To find out more about [Your Brand Here], call (650) 565-9653 or contact Wolf at Christy.wolf@yourbrandhere.com or visit Your Brand Here.


    Printing for PiE

    Marcia Yeates, owner of Inksmith Printing in Palo Alto and a Palo Alto High School parent, is proud that for many years her business has been printing all the materials used in PiE direct mailings, the PAUSD Teacher Grant mailings as well as PiE decals, bookmarks and notepads -- and all at a 15% discount, saving PiE over $1,000 annually.

    Yeates and her husband, Steve, have been supporting PAUSD schools through PiE and its predecessor organization, the Palo Alto Foundation for Education (PAFE) for over seven years.

    "Education makes a lifelong difference to each and every boy and girl. Our children benefit directly from the excellent instruction from Palo Alto schools," Yeates said. "Since we can't spend time in the classrooms, we can support PiE through discounts on printing, pro bono work and finding vendors willing to do the same. We believe in supporting our community and one of the best ways to do that is to support the schools."

    To contact Yeates or to find out more about Inksmith Printing, visit Inksmith Printing or call (650) 318-8033.

    Palo Alto Realtors, Loyal Supporters of Palo Alto Schools, Give $50,000 to PiE

    On Wednesday, April 11, the Palo Alto District of the Silicon Valley Association of Realtors (SILVAR) presented PiE with a check for just over $50,000 and feted members of PiE and the Palo Alto School District at a wine-and-hors d'oeuvres event at the Garden Court Hotel in Palo Alto.

    Michael Hall, a Realtor with Alain Pinel Realtors and co-chair of the Palo Alto Realtors Campaign for PiE, lauded the Palo Alto Unified School District for "the high quality of education that our children enjoy in Palo Alto."

    "As Realtors, we understand the value of good schools," Hall said. "Our children are the hope of our future and education is the key to their success in the wider world."

    The Palo Alto Realtors have been longstanding contributors to PiE as well as to its predecessor organization, the Palo Alto Foundation for Education (PAFE), having given more than $350,000 to these school fundraising groups over the past seven years.

    Susan Bailey, president of PiE, expressed gratitude to the Realtors -- both for their valuable financial boost to PiE and also for their demonstration of a "real community effort in support of our schools."

    Mary Frances Callan, PAUSD Superintendent noted that PAUSD was by all measures one of the top four unified school districts in California and, addressing the Realtors, said, "we couldn't do what we're doing without you."

    The Palo Alto Realtors have also pledged an additional $25,000 to PiE's Honor Your Teacher Campaign this fall.
    Hall can be reached at mhall@apr.com or (650) 543-1084.
