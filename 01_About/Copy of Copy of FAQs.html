Frequently Asked Questions 2009-2010

I. Questions Regarding Use of PiE Funds

What does PiE fund?
PiE funds essential staffing and program needs in our district to deepen and enrich the education of all students.  PiE’s goal is to ensure that our children continue to receive an excellent education beyond what is possible with public dollars.

PiE targets its funds, focusing on educational initiatives deemed high priority by principals, teachers, key district personnel, and parents. For each school level (elementary, middle, and high school), PiE supports three targets that have high impact and need. This approach allows PiE to focus on a few priority areas that support the PAUSD strategic plan.  Most school foundations throughout California use this collaborative method of targeted fundraising and they have been very successful with this approach.

For Elementary Schools, PiE Funds:

  • Classroom Support: aides, assistants, reading/math specialists, and teacher coaches who support and enrich all students’ learning  
  • Science Enrichment: programs and specialists who help science “come alive” for students through hands-on units
  • Arts Instruction: trained staff who teach Spectra Art and comparable creative arts

For Middle Schools, PiE Funds:

  • Academic Enrichment: additional courses and classroom support in video production, creative writing, music and biotechnology
  • Student Support: programs which address 6th and 8th grade transitions; academic guidance and support; and character development
  • Instructional Technology: mentors who assist teachers in applying technology to curriculum that enhances student learning

For High Schools, PiE Funds:

  • College and Career Counseling: staffing to support advising and resource programs which help all students plan wisely for the future  
  • Career/Technology Education: additional courses in engineering, media technology and performing arts 
  • Instructional Technology: mentors who assist teachers in applying technology to curriculum that enhances student learning

Are the targets just for one year or will the targets be the same every year?
These targets were based on high priority funding needs identified by the principals, along with input from the PAUSD online survey in April 2008, the PiE online survey in March 2009, and feedback from various teachers and parents across the district.  We anticipate that these funding needs will not change dramatically from year to year; however, they will be reviewed every year to be certain the targets are still appropriate.

How do the PiE targets fit with the new PAUSD strategic plan?
PiE has worked closely with Superintendent Skelly and members of the school board to ensure that PiE funding priorities are also high priorities in the strategic plan for the district.  For instance, college and career counseling, a PiE high school funding target is also a high priority item in the strategic plan (Strategic Initiative A2).  Middle school transition programs, another PiE funding target, is part of the strategic plan section A2.2.  PiE’s target funding of classroom support for elementary schools is part of the PAUSD strategic plan section A1.1 that has as its goal: “Improve K-12 curriculum articulation, coordination, and alignment to meet needs of the full range of students.”  To view the PAUSD strategic plan online, visit: http://pausd.org/community/board/downloads/presentations/07_08/StrategicPlan052708.pdf

Does this year’s fundraising affect children in the 2009-2010 (current) school year?
Every year, funds raised by PiE are distributed and spent in the following year. This approach ensures that the schools do not overspend on additional staffing until they know that the funds have been raised.  You may want to take this into consideration when deciding whether to support the Elementary School, Middle School or High School fund.  For example, if you have a current 5th grader, you may want to donate to the Middle School PiE campaign since the funds will be used in the following year.

Do PiE contributions go to the schools or to the district?
PiE contributions are allocated on a per student basis directly to schools, supporting the growth and development of every child in the district.

How are PiE funds distributed? 
PiE funds are distributed on a per-student basis throughout PAUSD schools.  For details on how much funding your child’s school will receive this year, and how the PiE funds are spent, visit the PiE website [click here, for online version] at www.papie.org.

How are PiE funds spent at each school?
PiE contributions to PAUSD go to each school’s site fund.  The principals can spend their PiE funds across the three target funding areas in the manner they deem most appropriate for their school, and sometimes consult with their Site Councils as to how best to use the funds.  PiE receives regular reporting on how these funds are being used.

Will the money raised still be distributed equally amongst all students?
For each PiE school fund (elementary, middle, and high school), contributions will be distributed to schools on a per-student basis as it has been in past years.

How is GATE addressed in these targets?
In PAUSD elementary and middle schools, the program model for GATE is differentiation within the mainstream classrooms.  PiE fundraising targets address GATE students in elementary schools by providing classroom aides, assistants and specialists “to foster more individualized learning and accommodate the full spectrum of learning abilities and styles.”  In middle schools, PiE funds target additional courses and enrichment activities that allow schools to preserve a wide range of rich offerings, such as video production, biotechnology and creative writing.  In high school, PiE funds target career tech education electives such as engineering and digital media that enable all students to try new and innovative areas of study.

For more information on GATE in PAUSD, check the district website at: http://www.pausd.org/parents/programs/gate/index.shtml.

II. Donation Questions

How much should I donate?
The request is $650 per student for the school year. However, recognizing the wide range of financial capabilities in our community, donations of any size are welcome and appreciated. While Palo Alto’s funding request is still lower than foundation requests from other neighboring school districts, such as Los Altos and Menlo Park, with the economic challenges of the past year, PIE is maintaining the same donation request as last year. Overall, the goal for 2009-2010 is to net $650 per child.

What if I can’t give $650 per student?
We realize that not all families will be able to give at this level.  Each family is encouraged to support the schools and every contribution continues to be greatly needed and appreciated.  For families needing more flexibility, it is possible to make monthly installments.

Isn’t Public Education supposed to be Free?
It is free, but over the years many programs have been eliminated and staff’s stretched. Foundations have emerged across the country to help meet our desire for great schools. Partners in Education is Palo Altos’ foundation established to support our local schools’ ability to deliver the type of hands-on education and programs that our community wants.

As background, California currently ranks 47 out of 50 states in spending per student based on Education Week’s January 2009 “Quality Counts” report.  California’s rank is expected to drop even lower with the impending state education budget cuts.  According to a recent Stanford study, K-12 education in California is underfunded by $17 Billion.¹ In the Palo Alto Unified School District spending per student is $1,000 less than the median spending per student in other top-performing school districts across the country, after adjusting for salary differences.²  PiE’s goal is to help offset the gap in spending on our children’s education, primarily by paying for additional and much-needed PAUSD teachers and staff. Palo Alto is not the only community compelled to raise private funds for public education. Since 1978 when Proposition 13 was passed, California has faced severe restrictions on school funding. As a result, in the past 31 years, school districts throughout California have relied more heavily on collaborations among schools, parents, businesses and community members to raise private funds to pay for critical school needs. Nearby school foundations in Menlo Park, Los Altos and Mountain View provide millions of dollars to support the schools in their districts. Indeed, there are over 600 school foundations throughout California that raised over $130 Million in 2007 to fund vital programs in their districts.³

How can I donate?
Donations can be made online via credit card or PayPal at http://www.papie.org/donPers.shtml , or by downloading the remit form on our website and sending a check or credit card information to: PiE, Department 33329, PO Box 39000, San Francisco, CA  94139-3329.  Also, each school has a box in the office where donations can be placed.  It is possible to make monthly installments or pledge contributions as well. PiE also accepts donations of stock or cars. Wire transfers can be coordinated with the PiE office.

You can donate to four different PiE funds: Elementary School, Middle School, High School, and Unrestricted.  Within each school-level fund, the amount collected will be distributed to schools on a per-student basis as it has been in past years.

How do I join the PiE Leadership Circle?
The PiE Leadership Circle honors families who show extraordinary commitment to supporting our schools financially. For families who donate $2,500 or more to PiE in a school year, there are several special thank-you opportunities as part of the PiE Leadership Circle.  These include group and individual discussions with the PAUSD Superintendent, and a year-end thank you cocktail party.  Families may qualify for the Leadership Circle through an outright gift, by more than one gift during the school year (with all gifts totaling $2,500 or more) or through an outright gift matched by a corporate matching gift (with a combined total of $2,500 or more). For more information, visit the PiE website: http://www.papie.org/donPersLead.shtml

Can my employer make a matching gift?
Yes. Donations go twice as far with matching gifts from employers.  If you are not sure whether your employer offers a matching gift program, you can check on our website at http://www.matchinggift.com/papie/.   On the PiE donation form, please fill out the matching gift information.  If you have any questions, please email donations@papie.org or call the PiE office at: (650) 329-3990.

Can I donate to a specific target program?
It is PiE’s policy to accept donations for one or more of the general funds: elementary, middle, high school or unrestricted.  If you are interested in donating to a specific program at your school or in the district, please contact Muneerah Merchant at mmerchant@papie.org, or 650-329-3990.

How can businesses and corporations support PiE?
The business community is vital to the success of PiE in fulfilling our fundraising goals.  In the 2008-09 school year, 22.5% of donations to PiE came from small, medium and large businesses and other donors in the extended non-parent community.  There are two ways in which businesses and the extended community can become a partner with PiE.  

  • Corporate Friend: Smaller businesses may become a Corporate Friend by contributing a minimum of $1,000.  
  • Corporate Partner: Larger businesses or individuals/community groups may become a Corporate Partner by contributing a minimum of $5,000.  

All deductions are tax-deductible.  In addition, Corporate Friends and Corporate Partners receive substantial exposure and goodwill within the Palo Alto community.  For more details, please visit the PiE website at http://www.papie.org/donCorp.shtml, or contact Muneerah Merchant, PiE Executive Director, at mmerchant@papie.org or 650.329.3990.

III. General Questions about PiE

What are PiE’s fundraising goals for the 2009-10 school year?
PiE’s goals are: (1) to contribute $2.57 Million to PAUSD this year from funds raised in direct appeal campaigns between August 2009 and February 2010, and (2) to give an additional $100,000 for teacher grants primarily through funds raised in the Realtor Campaign in the fall 2009 and the “Honor Your Teacher” campaign in April 2010.

How has the fundraising been going for PiE?
Since its inception in 2004-05, PiE has enjoyed an impressive growth spurt. Year over year PiE has grown, not only in dollars raised for schools, but also in the percentage of families and community businesses supporting the foundations, going from 27% enrolled family participation in 2005 to more than 45% in 2008-2009.

Is PiE still focused on staffing needs?
Yes, PiE remains the only nonprofit organization whose contributions can pay for additional and much needed PAUSD teachers and staff.

How do PiE, PTA and Bond Measure A funds work together?
Each organization allocates funds toward different, but complimentary, targets. PiE pays for additional staffing, PTA funds pay for materials, supplies, and community building and Bond Measure A funds support improvements to or new facilities.  PiE remains the only nonprofit organization whose contributions can pay for additional PAUSD teachers and staff.  And, because PiE is district-wide, it is able to attract donations from businesses and community members who want to support the school district as a whole.

How does the PiE teacher grant program work?
Two times a year PiE funds grants for PAUSD teachers – there is a submission process in the spring and fall.  The grants are for teacher programs or projects that promote innovation, creativity and excellence in areas such as special education, social studies, literacy, music, technology, art, science, mathematics, film and journalism. Teachers who are interested in applying should review the grant handbook at: http://www.papie.org/progHdbk.shtml.

What events does PiE sponsor or participate in throughout the year?
In 2009-2010, PiE will be actively involved in two main events: 

Italian Street Painting – August 22rd and 23th, 2009 from 10am to 6pm: Over the years, PiE has been the proud sponsor of the Italian Street Fair, part of the Palo Alto Festival of the Arts. Professional, amateur and student artists participate in this exciting event to “paint” large pictures on the Tasso Street pavement with colored chalk. There is also be a special section set aside for young artists aged 2-12 to paint 2’ x 2’ squares.  The proceeds raised from this event are used to support teacher grants and the arts in the Palo Alto Unified School District.

May Fete Town Fair – May XX, 2010 new date?: Every year, PiE continues the tradition first started by the All Schools Fund of thanking parents and the local community with the May Fete Town Fair.   It is held the first Saturday in May, immediately following the Palo Alto May Fete Parade organized by the city.  With the help of PiE Representatives, PiE Board members and high school students, PiE holds a traditional style fair with fun carnival games, food and drink.  We also invite local businesses to sponsor the Fair by renting booths for a day and they provide additional games and activities. 

Has teacher feedback been incorporated into PiE plans?
Absolutely!  PiE works with the teachers in many ways.  PiE representatives at each school often meet one-on-one with teachers or at teacher staff meetings.  PAUSD teachers who are interested in teacher grants for innovative new programs complete grant applications and often meet with PiE liaisons to discuss their plans.   PiE conducted a short survey of teachers at the end of the 2007-2008 school year to get their topline thoughts on PiE.  The responses were overwhelmingly positive:
“PiE is already such an amazing organization. Without them I do not think we would be able to do what we do with our students.”

What are PiE’s operating expenses?
PiE’s operating expenses continue to be extremely low.  In the 2008-09 fiscal year, operating expenses were 7.3%; allowing almost 93% of PiE donations to go directly to the schools.
