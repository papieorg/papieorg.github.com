What is PiE?

 

Palo Alto Partners in Education (PiE) is the only non-profit educational foundation devoted exclusively to raising money for all Palo Alto Unified School District (PAUSD) schools, and the only one whose contributions pay for additional and much needed PAUSD teachers and staff.  We partner with parents, the school district, and the community, to give all students the best education possible.

 

 

What are PiE’s fundraising goals for the 2010-2011 campaign?

 

PiE’s goal for the 2010-2011 Schools Campaign is to raise the funds needed to support critical staffing and programs at Palo Alto schools.  Specifically, we plan to raise $3.035 million for the Palo Alto Unified School District, a 4.65% increase from last year’s contribution.  Each year, we work closely with PAUSD to set our fundraising goals, in order to mitigate budget cuts and ensure continued excellence across district schools.  The funds we raise support carefully chosen target areas at each school level.  (Click here for more detailed information about PiE target areas.)

 

In addition, PiE’s goal for teacher grants remains at $100,000.  These funds encourage innovation and collaboration in teaching and are raised through the Realtor Campaign in the Fall and the Honor Your Teacher Campaign in the Spring.

 

 

Who runs PiE?

 

PiE is a non-profit educational foundation run by a Board of Directors of dedicated parent volunteers with professional expertise in a variety of areas, including fundraising, finance, development, business, and marketing.  Strong supporters of Palo Alto’s public schools, PiE Board Members understand that a public education is free, but a great education is not.  PiE employs a full-time Executive Director, Kathy Schroeder, who can be reached in the PiE district office at 650-329-3990 or kschroeder@papie.org.

 

 

Public school is supposed to be free.  Why should I contribute to PiE?

 

Unfortunately, public financing alone cannot provide the high quality education we expect in our community.  In order to maintain and build upon the academic excellence of our schools, which draws families to our neighborhoods and keeps property values high, we must seek additional funding. 

 

California’s spending on education has steadily decreased since the passage of Proposition 13, a consequence of the state’s tax-reform efforts.  Two types of school classifications resulted from this funding reduction: Basic Aid and Revenue Limited.  Revenue Limited districts do not raise enough taxes locally to meet the state minimums and therefore receive funding from the state based on the number of students enrolled.   Basic Aid districts (like Palo Alto) are perceived to have enough local tax revenue to meet minimum school funding and so get little or no state funding. 

 

As a Basic Aid district, we must rely on the generosity of local donors and ballot measures, in addition to federal and state funding, to augment property taxes and support our school district’s high standards of excellence. 

 

 

How does my contribution impact my child?

 

PiE is committed to an equitable distribution of funds across district schools on a per-pupil basis, so that all students may benefit.  In the Spring, together with each school principal, representative parent focus groups, and with input from the district, PiE sets its target goals for the following year.  Once these targets are set, principals work with their staff to allocate dollars appropriately.  PiE dollars directly support critical staff members that enrich education at the individual school level and support broader educational priorities set forth by the district’s strategic plan.  PiE donations can be directed towards elementary schools, middle schools, high schools, or remain unrestricted.  PiE target areas for 2010-11 are:

Middle Schools

High Schools

I just donated to my PTA.  Should I also donate to PiE?

 

Yes!  Both are critical funding sources for local schools.  As the only fundraising organization permitted to pay for salaries during the school day, PiE supports vital school “staff” such as classroom aides, who keep the teacher-student ratio low, art instruction, and science specialists at the elementary school level; staff support for technology mentoring, student guidance, and electives enrichment at the middle school level; and staff working in college and career counseling, student social and emotional support, and career/tech staff support at the high school level.

 

The PTA supports “stuff” such as classroom enrichment and supplies, school site improvements, playground equipment, student scholarships for field trips, and site-specific programs such as assemblies and parent-run community-building activities.

 

 

Didn’t we just pass Measure A, a parcel tax?  What about the recent Bond Measure?

 

Measure A passed in May 2010 with an historic 79% of the vote, sending a strong message of support from the community for Palo Alto schools.  Measure A imposes a $589 parcel tax for six years, which supports basic core academic programs such as reading, writing, math, and science across all schools in the district.

 

The June 2008 Strong Schools Bond funds are restricted to construction of new school facilities, including new classrooms to replace aging portables, renovating infrastructure, and providing an outstanding learning environment for all PAUSD students.

How much should I give?

 

Our suggested donation is $650 per child.  By comparison, suggested donations from neighboring school districts are: San Carlos, $750 per child; Los Altos, $800 per child; and Portola Valley, $1100 per child.  We set a recommended dollar amount that enables PiE to meet its target goals and fund essential staffing needs across the Palo Alto Unified School District. 

 

Every donation is appreciated and well-spent.  If you can, please consider joining our Leadership Circle for donors who give at the $2,500 level or higher.  For more information on joining our Leadership Circle, click here.

 

We ask that every family give at a level that is meaningful to you.  We hope to expand the participation rate of district families, which has increased steadily by 5% each year.

I’m unhappy with my individual teacher, principal, or district staff; why should I give to PiE?

 

If you are upset with any aspect of your child's education, please communicate your concerns to your teacher, principal and/or superintendent.  They all want to ensure your child has access to the best education possible, and is enjoying their school experience. Withholding a donation to PiE only increases the risk that class sizes will go up, specialists will be laid off, and essential staff will be cut.  When we give, every child benefits.
