Profile | Jim Anderson

Jim Anderson looks back on his long and varied career as a venture investor in Silicon Valley noting that, “Learning to adapt to unexpected outcomes, getting comfortable with doubt and helping others find their own success in an age of uncertainty is a very fulfilling approach to life.”

Anderson was born in Hollywood, CA to an “obstetrician dad and a PTA mom.”  He greatly values building relationships with people as well as the art of critical thinking, which he says he learned not from novels or history but in his chemistry class at public Arcadia High School, near Pasadena, where his teacher taught him to “question assumptions.”

“I was fascinated by telephone surveillance and spy technology.  I also played the baritone and trombone in the school marching band.  So, I just assumed I would try to go to USC and eventually get a job in the aerospace industry that was booming in southern California at the time,” he said. “Instead, my high school chemistry teacher said, ‘you’re an engineering and music kid, you should check out Purdue’s engineering courses and go explore life in the Midwest.’ Since that year Purdue was in the Rose Bowl and their amazing band was in the Rose Parade and broadcast on TV at half-time, I decided to go for it.”

At Purdue, Anderson focused on integrated circuit (IC) design and playing trombone and baritone in the music department jazz and marching bands. He graduated with a BS and MS in electrical engineering from Purdue. “Learning music and having strong high school electives in science changed my life,” Anderson said.

“Donating to Partners in Education (PiE) helps ensure Palo Alto’s students will have the same opportunities I did,” he said. “With PiE support, PAUSD offers so many electives that our kids can follow their own instincts to discover what they love.”

After graduation from Purdue, Anderson returned to southern California to work with Beckman Instruments developing custom ICs. “I worked on engineering everything from telephone surveillance to spy satellites and airline on-board theater systems to the Apollo trip to the moon,” he said.

Then, he was offered the opportunity to get an MBA at Stanford. “Going to business school and leaving engineering was either the best or the worst decision I ever made.  I still wonder, what might have been if I’d remained an engineer,” he mused. After getting his MBA, Hewlett Packard (HP) in Palo Alto offered him a job and Anderson came to Silicon Valley to stay.

After several years at HP, Anderson became a venture capitalist, joining an emerging Bank of America venture fund calledMerrill, Pickard, Anderson & Eyre.  “I never made it out to start my own company because it was much more fun helping other people to manage their businesses through the white water rapids of change. One of the most valuable things you can do in life is to help other people follow their passion and help their companies succeed.”

Anderson chose Palo Alto to raise his family primarily because “I felt a strong sense of connection to the local community with its strong schools, culture that values education, and clear sense of neighborhoods,” he said. “Great neighborhoods like those in Palo Alto make building relationships easier and allow us to help one another.”               

All four of his kids went to Palo Alto public schools. “Palo Alto schools do such a great job of taking advantage of parent and community volunteers and local organizations like the Junior Museum and Zoo and Theatreworks to offer an outstanding experience.  PAUSD offers a grounded, well-rounded education. Our kids loved going to school with the neighborhood kids, and you can’t get much better than PAUSD in terms of ranking for college.”

The schools gave Anderson the opportunity to develop his own community, as well.  “A lot of our close friendships here come from the relationships our kids first developed in kindergarten.  I still see many of these people on a regular basis, comparing notes on kids, sports and how the kids could really take advantage of school opportunities.” 

After founding Foundation Capital and backing entrepreneurs for several years, Anderson founded Legacy Ventures in 1999 to tap into the financial power of venture capital and combine it with an extensive philanthropic community.  All principal and profits earned by Legacy Partners is donated to the investors’ chosen charity. “I had a personal medical awakening in my life and it made me realize that people who are truly happy and successful in the valley are not in it only for the money – they are in it because they are passionate about building a successful entity, be it for profit or not-for-profit.” 

Anderson became a significant donor to PiE when he recognized it was a well-run non-profit foundation that benefits an entire community.  “What’s unique about PiE is that you have some of the best-organized people running it.  They ensure that money is not just handed over to the district, but designated thoughtfully to meet specific goals, be it another counselor, more music support, or alternative curriculum.  This kind of execution is unusual for a non-profit that is supporting such a big organization as the public school system.”

Anderson’s positive outlook extends to his personal life, where he loves spending time as a father and claims that “it is never too late to become a decent snow skier.” He also loves boats and water sports, and is passionate about traveling the world. “The great thing about living in Palo Alto is that your neighbors will keep an eye on your house while you’re gone and you’re happy to see them when you get back!

Jim Anderson has been a venture capitalist for more than 25 years.  He was a founding partner in both Merrill Pickard Anderson & Eyre, and in Foundation Capital.  He has fours children, one working at Salesforce.com, another in college and two in a PAUSD high school.  Jim holds an MBA from the Stanford Graduate School of Business and a BSEE and an MSEE from Purdue University.  He does angel investing, mentors students in entrepreneurial classes at Stanford, plays the trombone, enjoys his water boats and toys and is currently planning his next trip overseas.
