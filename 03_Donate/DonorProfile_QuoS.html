Profile | Dr. Stacey Quo

There are two things that Dr. Stacey Quo firmly believes can transform the lives of children: a great public education and the power of a beautiful smile. When we visited Dr Quo at her orthodontic practice on a Thursday afternoon, the office was surprisingly empty. “We’re getting ready for afternoon visits from kids who benefit from one-at-a-time appointments, a quiet office and supportive surroundings,” Dr. Quo said.

Besides her real passion for the “whole experience” of her patients and her kind nature, what shines about Dr Quo, is her dedication to improving public education for all of Palo Alto’s children. A PiE Business Partner in Education at the Summa Cum Laude level, Dr. Quo donates to PiE to sustain high quality programs and electives in the schools, including strong guidance programs, the arts, and music. A violinist since childhood, Dr. Quo’s love of music recently included a stint in the Palo Alto Chamber Orchestra. She can also be found out kayaking on the San Francisco Bay and cooking up hearty meals for her large, close-knit family.

Dr. Quo attended public schools in Cincinnati, Ohio, majoring in bio-medical engineering at Northwestern. She was greatly influenced by her professor father, who emphasized contributing to community education, and has been teaching orthodontics for 20 years at UCSF dental school, her professional alma mater. She also teaches at Stanford in the Sleep Apnea clinic, where she helps patients “open their airways” through her understanding of facial development.

She lives across the street from her pediatric dentist brother, Dr. Brian Quo, and her practice Mid-Peninsula Orthodontics just celebrated its 15-year milestone in Palo Alto. “I love this community; people here are so involved in the schools, the outdoors and city activities and really embrace the concept that it only takes one person to make a difference.” That’s Dr. Quo’s mantra. Most night and weekends she can be found checking in at the office and following up on “her kids”, who make up 60% of her practice. “I love orthodontics because the results are so visual,” Dr. Quo says “It’s so fulfilling to see a shy teenager blossom and feel good about themselves when they love their new smile!”
