Donor Profile: We Give!


Profile | Pam and John Markevitch

As an alumnus of Palo Alto schools (Palo Verde, JLS, Gunn), John Markevitch has seen first hand the transformation of California’s educational curriculum over the last several decades and its impact on the traditional structure of the classroom.

"Today, my kids are learning a lot more things sooner than I did, from kindergarten on," Markevitch said. "At my daughter’s middle school, the science curriculum seems a lot more advanced. She’s covering cell structure that I remember learning in AP Bio. My son did a sheep heart dissection this week at his elementary school, and a parent volunteer who is a pediatrician said he didn't do that until medical school."

John’s wife Pam Markevitch agrees. "Our kids are learning more than what we were taught., I’m an aide in the classroom and my position is funded by Partners in Education (PiE). It’s so helpful for the teachers to have aides, which frees them up to teach all the material to the kids. If I’m doing small group environment or prep work to support the class, I am one more person to really help deliver a more advanced curriculum and allow the teachers to have more one-on-one time with students. I see first-hand all the things PiE does for schools."

The Markevitch’s are raising two children in Palo Alto, a daughter in seventh grade and a son in fifth. They love the Palo Alto experience for their children.. "I think the Palo Alto schools are great," Markevitch said. "PiE funds gives all our kids a fuller, richer curriculum in schools." They also have a broader range of electives from which to choose, many of which are due to funding which is a great help in our schools. As a result of PiE, education in Palo Alto hasn’t suffered as much post Proposition 13 as some other areas in California.

Pam and John are proud to give to PiE at the $800 per student suggested donation level via the installment program. John said, "It’s frankly so much easier. The installment program allows us to give at the ask without the pain of one giant check. Also, knowing that PiE gives the district the money at the end of the campaign, it all goes to the district at the same time no matter when we give."

Pam added, "Installments help us with budgeting, too. The process is pretty painless. Once you set it up it just gets billed to your credit card every month. Plus John’s employer matches as well, which really helps us increase our gift to schools."

John observed, "Parents are incredibly involved in education in Palo Alto. Our daughter’s science teacher assigns five minutes of additional homework every night where the kids have to explain their class work to their parents. This discussion engages us as parents, helps us relearn some of what we've forgotten, and helps solidify her learning by explaining to us."

Pam added, "Our elementary school has Discovery Day where parent and community members teach something they love to kids, from fencing to football to cell bio to making candles to knots. What’s great about that is it’s the kind of thing that introduces kids to something that may become a passion, sparking something in them that they will carry forward.” Pam also believes that the arts are crucial. “Art is really important because it opens kids’ minds in ways that they might not get through traditional curriculum. In elementary schools the arts are done so well through Spectra Art which is fully supported by PiE."

John concluded, "School funding, especially from the state, is unreliable. PiE plays the role of making important school programs reliable anyway because they pay for things that otherwise might not continue. They would be sorely missed if not available. That’s why we’re so happy to support PiE in our schools."
