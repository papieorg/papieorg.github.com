Donor Profile: We Give!


Profile | Hideko Sakamoto and Vijay Tella

Hideko Sakamoto and Vijay Tella are passionate about kids’ education. “For the past ten years,” said Vijay, “we have been investing in building or renovating schools in India. You can make your dollar go far in India, and we have seen some tangible results from the money we have given.”

The good feelings the couple realized from benefitting children in India, however, was nothing compared to their experience as their own children entered elementary school in Palo Alto. Hideko said, “I was volunteering in the kindergarten classroom and the teacher did an exercise where she asked all the kids to put stickers by their favorite activities at school. She had listed things like recess, PE, lunch, math, and reading, but the activity that got the most stickers, that the kids loved the most, was art, even more than recess. Art at our school is funded entirely by Partners in Education. That’s when I realized that we wanted to give to PiE.”

Added Vijay, “We believe in public education. The diversity that our kids encounter is important and more reflective of the real world. Palo Alto schools have a great reputation, so it was easy to decide to live here and send our kids to school here. We knew that the kids would get good academics, but felt it was important too that they have a well-rounded education. PiE provides the programs that make our kids’ education so complete. PiE funds all of the art at our school, science, and the aides that help our teachers every day.”

In talking with their children’s teachers, Hideko and Vijay realized the crucial role of the classroom aides. “Our teachers are doing so much for our kids, working very hard to give them all a great classroom experience. The aides who help them make them that much more effective. Support from aides can help keep the good teachers in our system and helps give our kids a great education experience. You can’t take these things—aides, arts, sciences—away and still have the same holistically good system,” said Vijay.

Hideko added, “We’ve got two very different kids, but they both thrive at school. They get so much out of the projects they get to do because of PiE. They look forward to school, have great friends and play not only with classmates but with kids older than they are. Our school has a very supportive environment, very focused on kids, with high expectations. PiE adds so much to that environment.”

Vijay pointed out that PiE is crucial for Palo Alto schools and is likely to become more so in the future. “Investing in schools in India really opened our eyes, especially to the impact of the budget and funding challenges. Donations make a tangible difference for schools, which is why we’re involved with PiE. I hope parents realize that the budget situation is unlikely to get better in the short term. I think it’s important that everyone support PiE because the programs that PiE provides have a big positive impact, and if they go away that will have an equally large negative effect on schools. Plus, there is nothing like the feeling you get from knowing that you are helping your kids and others. It’s immediate and quite positive.”

Hideko Sakamoto went to Gakushuin University in Tokyo and then transferred to Palo Alto with Tibco. She retired after her children were born. Vijay Tella attended the Indian Institute of Technology and then received a master’s degree from UCLA. He too was an early employee at Tibco. Most recently, he was the CEO of Qik, which he sold to Skype last year. The couple has two children in Palo Alto schools.
