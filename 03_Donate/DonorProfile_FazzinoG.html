Donor Profile: We Give!


Profile | Gary Fazzino

Former Palo Alto mayor Gary Fazzino grew up attending Palo Alto public schools. He says, “I’m a graduate of PAUSD and I received an absolutely wonderful education here. Palo Alto has an outstanding public school system. I arrived here at age 13 (from the East Coast) and remember feeling overwhelmed by the diversity of courses, great teachers, and intense community focus on education.”

Palo Alto, says, Gary, has a long-standing tradition of embracing its schools and its youth. “This community has such a focus on education; it really makes Palo Alto unique. For years, the community has sustained its schools. In 1987, we signed a ground-breaking agreement for the city to lease old school sights—an innovative city-school partnership demonstrating tremendous support for our schools and our children. And our school system remains one of the best in the country as a result.”

Gary is also a longtime PiE donor. “There are a lot of reasons why I give to PiE. I was the first president of Palo Alto’s first education foundation (a predecessor to PiE) back in, 1986, and have long supported community-based groups that, in turn, support our outstanding schools. I think PiE is such a tangible way for the entire community to help maintain our great public education system. Especially as the challenges facing school funding grow, and there’s less support from the state and less flexibility to help locally, PiE is crucial.”

Gary believes that PiE is an essential ingredient to ongoing PAUSD school success. “I think it’s great that our schools are surrounded by a committed, passionate, dedicated, hard-working group of people that has made PiE such a success. I am overwhelmed by the support shown for schools shown by this community. And PiE is not just paying for field trips—it’s providing real dollars and addressing real gaps in the district’s funding and programs.”

Now a father, Gary plans to send his kids to his neighborhood elementary school next year—and to continue his contributions to PiE. “Of all the groups that I support, I can’t think of any group that provides a higher degree of value for contributions.”

Gary hopes that others, including those who do not have kids in the schools, realize the importance of supporting kids and youth. “The biggest challenge in California is its disconnected fiscal structure. Until local districts have more tools to raise money locally, foundations like PiE are critical for maintaining schools. I hope that everyone will join Annette and me in supporting schools through PiE.”

Gary Fazzino is a longtime resident and former City Council member and Mayor of Palo Alto. He is a Tall Tree award winner and has contributed on many Palo Alto boards and committees. He currently serves as Vice President of Government Affairs for Applied Materials. Along with his wife, Annette, Gary looks forward to sending his twins, Matthew and Julia, through Palo Alto public schools.
