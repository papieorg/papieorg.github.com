Profile | Clint Smith

Partners in Education has true partners in Clint Smith, a Gunn alum, and his wife Elizabeth. The couple met in law school, at Berkeley. After a stint on the East Coast, and ultimately, in Australia, where they took their preschoolers for the experience of living abroad, they headed back to their native California and extended family.

Proponents of the Palo Alto education this couple has happily sent all three of their children to Palo Alto schools. In addition to being PiE Leadership Circle supporters, Clint has served on the PiE chair team for as long as their children have attended the school, and is co-chair for his third year in 2011-12.

On the topic of public schooling, Clint believes that a community has a primary responsibility to educate its children. Elizabeth points out that Palo Alto is a rare example of excellent publically-funded schools, especially in an economic climate beset with cutbacks. Both recognize that PiE makes a huge difference in filling the funding gap.

Given the direct impact that PiE funds have on the classroom and the students, Clint feels that every family should be making a meaningful gift to PiE. “I haven’t met a family yet who hasn’t been touched by PiE.”

Clint adds: “Most of my PiE fundraising work involves interacting with parents of other students, and I'm inspired by their generosity. But I'm equally inspired by all the PiE contributors from the community who have no children in our schools: the educators, realtors, grandparents and others in the community who support PiE because they want our children to have a richer educational experience. PiE is fortunate to enjoy such a broad base of support."

PiE’s excellent participation rates for donor support are a testament to Clint’s vigorous volunteer recruitment. “ I think he brings in more dads than might otherwise join in,” explains Elizabeth. The Smiths make supporting PiE a family affair, as their own children pitch in on PiE Donation Days, interacting with students and their parents, encouraging support.

Our kids are fortunate to have families like the Smiths getting behind the cause of education, and bringing so many others along.
