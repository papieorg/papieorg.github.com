Profile | Jennifer Buenrostro

Like many realtors, Jennifer Buenrostro started off doing something else. Her hard work ethic and steely discipline were first forged on pointe shoes performing as a professional ballerina with one of the Bay Area’s top ballet companies, the Cleveland San Jose Ballet.

“From the time I was five I knew I wanted to dance,” Buenrostro said. “The dedication and focus that was required of me to reach that goal has helped me later in life with my real estate career.”

After six years with the company, Buenrostro decided to leave ballet to attend college.  “Dancing professionally was an amazing experience, but I always knew I wanted to go to college,” she said.  Buenrostro again landed a coveted position, this time at Stanford, where she obtained a degree in Economics. She started her second career in the financial services industry, but then moved into real estate.

“I chose real estate because I watched my mom, Nancy Mott, who has a wealth of experience and success from 25 years as a Real Estate agent excel in it and felt like it was suited to me as well,” Buenrostro explained.  “Working closely with my clients to help them achieve their goals, and ultimately change their lives, gives me great pleasure.”

In 2006, Buenrostro secured her first position as a realtor in the Palo Alto office of Alain Pinel Realtors. Partnering with her mom and working with other top agents in the office, Buenrostro quickly excelled and was in the top 20% of agents in her office after her first year.  “My business has increased every year since then, and is now largely based on referrals,” she said.

Buenrostro has two boys, one in fourth grade and one in eighth.  Both attend Palo Alto public schools.  Buenrostro said, “We’ve had a fantastic experience with this district, which has really worked well for my children and their varying needs.”

This year, Buenrostro chose to make Palo Alto Partners in Education (PiE) her signature charity.  She donated through the Business Partners in Education Program at the Summa Cum Laude level to support Palo Alto schools, the kids, and the community.  “I live here.  I work here.  I am rooted in this community and it’s necessary for me to not just give back but also support this circle.  I know it’s a worthy cause,” she explained. One main reason Buenrostro thinks her business is flourishing is that the schools are so good.  “Ninety percent of my sales are in Palo Alto and probably eighty percent of those buyers want Palo Alto because of the schools."

The Buenrostro family regularly attends ballet, symphony and jazz concerts in Palo Alto and around the Bay Area and on top of homework and the boy’s activities, they still manage to eat dinner together almost every single night. “Balancing work and family life is very important to me,” she said  "It feels great to be a supporter at this level, fulfilling my goals of providing for my family and this community.”

Buenrostro noted that clients appreciate Palo Alto’s very small town feel, where kids can walk to school and bike all around safely.  She believes that many Palo Alto residents share the same goal, to raise kids well and send them to great public schools. “Many of my clients are like-minded.  They put education first and some make sacrifices so that they can buy a home here.  I like that shared priority with my clients, and I appreciate their decision from a parent’s point of view as well.  I would not want to be a realtor anywhere else.”

Jennifer Buenrostro has lived in the Bay Area since 1991.  After a career as a ballet dancer, she worked for several years in the financial services industry before she became a Real Estate agent for Alain Pinel in 2006.  A parent of two, Buenrostro focuses on Palo Alto in her work and on the Palo Alto Unified School District for volunteering and working on various school PTAs boards.  Buenrostro holds a degree in economics from Stanford.
