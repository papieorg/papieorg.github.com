Profile | Steve Young

Ex-49er Steve Young and his family live in Palo Alto and are strong supporters of Palo Alto Partners in Education. “Palo Alto is a great place, the proximity to Stanford and the richness of life in town.  The sidewalks and trees are very like the East Coast where I’m from. We love the diversity and the culture--there are so many draws,” says Steve.

One of those draws is the Palo Alto Unified public schools. “My kids are in public school in the district because I’m a product of public schools. I came from one of the best public schools in the east (Greenwich, CT) and I wanted the same for my children. I think Palo Alto offers the same kind of quality in public schools.”

Supporting PiE, for the Youngs, was a natural fit.  Steve says, “We have been longtime PiE supporters because we understand the realities of public schooling especially under budget crunches, and we are not willing to stand on the sidelines. We’ve got to be fiscally and emotionally and physically involved in making sure the education of our children is primary. It’s part of it all. You have to work to make it a superior educational experience.”

And Steve is a fan of Partners in Education. He says, “I’m grateful for the opportunity to give to PiE and grateful that PiE exists. Sometimes there’s not that obvious a place to give money to do what you want. I’m glad to have money work efficiently and effectively to support education in Palo Alto schools. I want the funds to be spent on the ground to augment the learning experience of our children, which is what PiE does. It’s a great thing to have PiE.”

While the Youngs are pleased with the quality of education their kids are receiving in Palo Alto, they are concerned about the future, especially given the state’s budget challenges. “I hope that people recognize that public education in this state will be under a tremendous amount of strain in the near term and maybe long term. To maintain this kind of education, PiE is going to have to become a part of your life.  Public education is state funded and it’s severely under water. I hope people notice and give to keep our schools strong.”

Steve Young was a star quarterback for the San Francisco 49-ers, leading the team to a Super Bowl victory in 1994. Steve was inducted into the Pro Football Hall of Fame in 2005. Since retiring from football, Steve has worked in private equity, started the Forever Young Foundation, and worked in sports broadcasting. Married with four children, Steve is an active parent in Palo Alto, and can often be found ferrying his children to schools, camps, and other activities.
