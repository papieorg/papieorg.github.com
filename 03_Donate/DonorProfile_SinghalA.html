Profile | Shilpa and Amit Singhal

Shilpa and Amit Singhal moved to Palo Alto ten years ago, when Amit joined then-fledgling Google. Shilpa says, “We chose Palo Alto for its great schools and great community. People are well-educated here.” Adds Amit, “People are very involved in the schools and very friendly—it’s a great place to raise kids. There’s a saying that, ‘It takes a village,’ and this is the village that I’d rather raise my kids in.”

The Singhals believe deeply in a public school education. “Public schools are critical to a child’s development. We both went to public schools. The variety of children is so great and beautiful. You meet a lot more of the world in a public school. We want to raise our kids to be successful in the real world and public schools are a microcosm of what the kids will face in the real world,” says Amit.

And the Singhals are proud, long-time PiE supporters. Amit says, “PiE does great things. It provides all the extra programs that we see in school.” Shilpa adds, “Spectra Art was the first thing I learned about PiE and it’s wonderful. When I came to this country I was worried that my kids would not learn as much as in India, but then we started kindergarten with Spectra art and aides. It was phenomenal. PiE rounds out an already well-rounded education system. It enriches our kids’ education and that enrichment is critical for our children. PiE is a great organization that completes an already great school district.”

Shilpa and Amit agree that we all should become Partners in Education. “The thing that people often forget is that it indeed takes all of us to have a city as beautiful as Palo Alto and schools as good. The more people who participate in our community and in our kids’ schools, the better we will all be together. So we hope folks give to PiE and participate in every opportunity they can: in our schools and in our community, volunteering and donating.

Raised in India, Shilpa and Amit both attended Cornell, where Shilpa received two master’s degrees and Amit a Ph.D. Amit oversees Google search, coaches Little League, and truly enjoys spending time with family here in Palo Alto. Shilpa enjoys volunteering at schools and the luxury of being with her children. Both say they would change nothing: “It has truly been a joyous journey.”
