Profile | Anoop and Rashimi Goyal

Anoop and Rashmi Goyal appreciate the education that their daughters are receiving from the Palo Alto Unified School District. "We love the Palo Alto schools. The other students come from families that value and emphasize education and thereby provide a great peer group. The teachers are very competent and caring. Together, this creates an environment at the school that is great for learning. Our daughters are going into first and fourth grade and enjoy going to school."

Anoop and Rashmi, originally from India, are both software professionals. They did their schooling in India and attended college in the US at Stanford and the University of Illinois at Urbana Champaign, respectively. Rashmi has been employed at Oracle for seventeen years while Anoop has tried his hand at several startups and at big companies in the Valley.

When their children started school, they both wanted to be involved in the school as volunteers. Rashmi has been a regular room parent and Anoop chose PiE as his volunteer role. "I was looking to get involved at school and a friend told me about the opportunity to work with PiE. I thought it was great! PiE has good people working together for a great cause."

Rashmi is a big believer in the benefits provided by the programs that PiE donors help fund. "PiE is very helpful for schools. It gives the schools extra money to create the programs that provide a well-rounded education for our kids. It’s a very worthwhile cause."

Anoop adds, "We love the concept of PiE. PiE is our community coming together to provide the push and those extra resources that are needed. PiE helps everyone invest together in our schools. It gives everyone the chance to get engaged and involved." Both their daughters love programs funded by PiE. Their eldest daughter is a big fan of art while the younger daughter loves science. The elder daughter’s paintings have been selected for display at various places and the credit goes to PiE

Anoop says, "We both work full-time and our family has a very busy life, but supporting PiE financially and through volunteering is important to us. It’s such a great way to contribute to the community. There are very few things in life that are more important than our kid’s education."
