Profile | Megan Swezey Fogarty

Palo Alto Tall Tree Outstanding Citizen Award winner Megan Swezey Fogarty was born and raised in Palo Alto. Megan’s father, Charles Lawrence Swezey, now age 88, moved with his wife to Palo Alto after serving in Japan during World War II. Swezey attended Stanford Law School on the GI Bill and put down roots in Palo Alto, where Megan’s oldest brother entered kindergarten in 1954 at Van Auken School (since closed).

Megan says, "My parents’ arrival in Palo Alto is the postwar story: the quality of the schools and the community’s commitment to education was evident even back then." Megan’s parents raised nine children in Palo Alto. All attended PAUSD schools from kindergarten through 12th, including Van Auken, Greendell, all four middle schools (including Wilbur, now closed), Cubberley, and Paly.

Megan has been a volunteer and leader with a large number of local nonprofits, including Leadership Mid-Peninsula, Foundation for a College Education, First Congregational Church Nursery School, Youth Community Service, Palo Alto Community Child Care, Adolescent Counseling Services, Palo Alto Community Fund, Family Resource Center Implementation Team, City of Palo Alto Comprehensive Plan Committee, Mothers' Symposium, YWCA of the Mid-Peninsula, First Congregational Church of Palo Alto, Palo Alto Partners in Education, and Partners in School Innovation. She has also supported local sports teams, girls scout troops, and campaigns.

Megan attributes her commitment to community to her father. "One thing my father instilled in me was his commitment to public institutions as a core value of our civil society. My dad was an attorney with the State of California the majority of his career, so public service was a critical part of his life. He was also part of the PTA at Van Auken School and volunteered at church and with Boy Scouts."

And the story doesn’t stop with Megan and her siblings. It continues to the next generation. Megan’s children and her nieces and nephews from four other Swezey families have grown up and are currently attending PAUSD schools, attending, among them, El Carmelo, Walter Hays, Addison, Ohlone, Duveneck, JLS, Jordan, and Paly.

Megan says, "I wax a little nostalgic to the day when our state invested in K-12 education. Because I’ve worked with Joe Simitian and follow K-12 education at the state level, it’s deeply troubling how we have lost our commitment to making K-12 education the best it can be in California.

"That being said, my kids’ education is fabulous, undoubtedly better than mine. That can be directly attributed to PiE and the support people give to our schools. When I see the electives, the enrichment, and the counseling, I am thrilled. It’s become clear that PiE is crucial for sustaining these core activities. My kids are touched by donations to PiE every day."

A member of PiE’s Advisory Council and former school site rep, Megan has been a PiE supporter since day one. She says, "If we want public education to be sustained in Palo Alto, PiE is our mechanism for doing it."
