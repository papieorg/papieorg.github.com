Profile | Juliana Lee

Every era in Palo Alto real estate has a star who embodies its ethos, and in this moment Juliana Lee is that agent.  Multiple listing services (MLS) records show Ms. Lee having completed more real estate sales in Palo Alto than any other agent.  Indeed, according to the nation’s largest real estate firm, Keller Williams, Juliana Lee was their number-two producing real estate agent worldwide last year.

But the numbers don’t tell the full story. With Asians representing 65 percent of new homebuyers in Palo Alto, Ms. Lee, a 1978 émigré from Taipei, Taiwan, inspires appreciation and respect amongst her clients with her narrative of hard work and ceaseless reinvention of her career before finding the best fit and mega-success at Keller Williams in Palo Alto.

“Palo Alto is the ideal real estate market because of the community’s devotion to public education and its many residents building new businesses,” Ms. Lee says, adding that she shares the commitment to excellent public schools.  In March of this year, she gave Palo Alto Partners in Education (PiE), the city’s public education foundation, a check for $50,000, the largest single donation in its history.    

To achieve her goal of benefitting as many children as possible, Ms. Lee chose to donate to Palo Alto public schools because she wants to help the schools provide an outstanding education to local children.  She elected to make an unrestricted gift to PiE because it benefits every child in the K-12 school district.  Ms. Lee, especially, wants to lead the way among Asians in local philanthropy and hopes the Asian families who move to Palo Alto mainly to send their children to local schools will follow her example to help keep the schools strong.

Ms. Lee says there is a huge upside for the community from Palo Alto’s newest Asian residents.  “Asians value education and family above all else,” she says.  “As they assimilate into the community, residents will see the importance they place on great schools and supportive families.  Asian families help one another get through life’s ups and downs.”

Ms. Lee's own daughter, Jade, attended Walter Hays and Gunn High School and is thankful for the excellent interactive education Jade received compared to Ms. Lee’s own experience as a public school student in Taipei.  “The parent involvement in education here is unbelievable,” she says.  Ms. Lee adds that her daughter’s classroom “had full-time parent volunteers who had retired early as successful high-tech entrepreneurs.  You don’t find that everywhere in public schools!”  she exclaims.

Ms. Lee adds that in Taiwan, as in Palo Alto, parents have extremely high expectations for their children’s performance as students.  The difference, she says, is that in Taipei and much of Asia, teaching methods are largely based on student memorization of facts and preparing for exams rather than on interactive projects, imaginative play and group work, which were common in her daughter’s experience in Palo Alto schools. 

For Ms. Lee, like many immigrants to the United States, success did not come easily. She attributes her achievements not only to her determination to always be number one at whatever she does, but also to her graduate education in the United States and its supportive culture for women entrepreneurs.  Like her hero Beethoven – who, she noted, created soaring symphonies despite being deaf – she persevered, overcoming the odds to find her way.

Freshly graduated in 1981 with an MBA from College of Notre Dame and law degree from Fu Jen University in Taiwan, Ms. Lee tried being an accountant for a small company, a stockbroker in Vancouver, Washington and finally a real estate agent. “I had a series of bad bosses and work situations that ranged from boring to unethical and realized that the advantage of real estate is that you work for yourself, achieving greater rewards for diligent work, creative ideas, and high ethics,” Ms. Lee says.  

“I also realized that in order to succeed in real estate, you have to set yourself apart and build a unique brand,” she says.  Ms. Lee chose…to wear hats.  “It may seem trivial but people remember me and look for the hat,” she admits. At the moment, Ms. Lee is crowned in a stunning mint green, sinamay up-brim, trimmed with ribbon. Ms. Lee says she enjoys working with clients and handling all the negotiations to provide excellent client service rather then running her own name-brand company.

Ms. Lee adds that her successful formula is also based on teamwork. She praises her six team members who provide the logistics, client management, research, legal and office support as well as Keller Williams agents, who brainstorm and provide feedback to one another.

Nonetheless, it’s getting harder than ever to buy a home in Palo Alto, Ms. Lee murmurs, noting that all-cash deals are becoming the new normal. One grateful client of Ms. Lee, a Twitter executive, is sitting in the waiting room. “He’s so thrilled that we found him a Palo Alto home, he stopped by after work and is going to teach us how to use Twitter to create social media campaigns,” she proudly exclaims. 

Still, Ms. Lee looks forward to the day when she will have time to indulge her passion to travel around the U.S. without a phone, play flute and violin, and cook gourmet meals in the dream house kitchen she built in Palo Alto several years ago. Her partner, Jeff Keller, observes drily that, at the moment, Ms. Lee’s vacations are spent “on the phone with clients.  She finds it more satisfying to get each one of them their Palo Alto dream house than to spend time in her own dream home or vacationing!”
